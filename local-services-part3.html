The Local Paid Goal

Be Where Intention Meets Opportunity

Every choice in a local paid program — campaign structure, keyword pattern, location targeting, ad copy — has one job: send high-intent traffic to the client's site at the moment a real local buyer is searching. The reason this matters more in local than in eCommerce is budget reality.

💰

Benefits of Local Paid

Enhanced visibility and relevance to local audiences. Attraction of new customers through location-based searches. Increased offline conversions by driving foot traffic to physical stores. Brand recognition and trust within the target market.

📍

What Defines Local Paid

A digital marketing strategy using location targeting and keyword modifiers to reach an audience within specific geographic areas. The location is the intent — not a filter applied after the fact.

🎯

Our Goal Per Account

Our paid media strategy is personalized for each business we serve. No template campaigns. Every account gets a structure tuned to its actual service area, services, and budget.

Section 1

The Three-Campaign Local Structure

The OBx local paid framework is built on three parallel campaigns. Each captures a different search behavior. Together they make sure the client shows up no matter how the buyer types their query.

Local Geo Modified

Priority

Location Targeting

Specific cities or towns client services (e.g., Lawn Mowing Houston, Lawn Mowing Cypress).

Why It's Priority

Priority campaign due to high intent — allocate most of the budget here.

Why It Works

Broad match modified KW Type catches varies with and without the location modifier but have the tailored ad for the area they are in.

Example Keyword

lawn mowing houston

National Geo Modified

Captures Off-Area Intent

Structure

Keywords, Ad Groups and Ads are an exact duplicate of "Local Geo Modified" campaign.

Difference

Location targeting set to entire United States and excludes the cities or towns that the "Local Geo Modified" campaign is targeting.

Why It Works

Often times, users can be searching for a need outside of the service area. They could be searching from another city/state or on behalf of someone else. We don't want to miss out on their need even if they are outside the target location when executing the search.

Example Keyword

lawn mowing houston (shown to a searcher in Dallas)

Near Me

Local Intent

Keyword Pattern

Leverage "Near Me" keyword modifier instead of city, town or state (e.g., "Lawn Mowing Near Me").

Location Targeting

Specific cities or towns client services (same as "Local Geo Modified" campaign).

Why It Works

Intent & Proximity-driven. 85% of these searches are from a mobile device AND 76% of customers who use their phones to conduct a local search will visit a nearby business within 24 hours. A space your client needs to be in.

Example Keyword

lawn mowing near me

💡 Why Three Campaigns, Not One

Each campaign has a different ad and a different bidding logic. The Local Geo Modified is where most spend lives — these are buyers actively searching with location intent. The National Geo Modified catches the out-of-area searcher (a parent in Dallas looking for a service for their kid in Houston) — different bid context, smaller spend. The Near Me campaign catches the device-location-driven searcher who hasn't typed a city — same target geography as Local Geo, but a different keyword. Lumping them into one campaign would muddy the data and waste budget.

Section 3

Keyword Strategy by Campaign Type

Keyword construction is simple once the campaign structure is set. The pattern is consistent across campaigns — just swap the modifier.

CampaignKeyword PatternExample
Local Geo ModifiedProduct/service keyword + location modifier"lawn mowing houston"
National Geo ModifiedSame keywords as Local Geo (duplicate ad group structure)"lawn mowing houston"
Near MeProduct/service keyword + "Near Me" modifier"lawn mowing near me"
⚠️ ZIP Code Targeting — A Note

We have not seen as great results from targeting zip codes. Stick with cities, towns, and regions for both keyword construction and location targeting. ZIP-level targeting tends to be too narrow and can suppress impression volume to the point of starving the campaign.

Section 4

Negative Keywords — Proactive Discipline

For local accounts on small budgets, negative keywords are not a maintenance task — they are an upfront discipline. Build them before you go live, not after you've burned budget on the wrong traffic.

🚫 Campaign Level — Exclude Irrelevant Geographies

  • Exclude irrelevant states or cities at the campaign level
  • States: include fully spelled out (Texas) + abbreviation (TX)
  • Pro tip: get this list from Google or ChatGPT to make sure it's complete

🎯 Ad Group Level — Prevent Internal Overlap

  • Prevent overlap by excluding locations targeted in other ad groups
  • Example: if "Audiologist Port Jefferson" is in one ad group, negate "Port Jefferson" in the others to avoid self-competition
💡 Why This Matters at $4K/Month

A campaign without aggressive negative keywords on a small budget will absolutely burn through that budget on traffic from states you don't service, neighboring cities outside the client's reach, and overlap across ad groups within your own account. Spending one hour building a complete negative list at launch saves weeks of cleanup later — and protects the budget that pays the client's leads.

Section 5

Ad Copy & Extensions

Ad copy for local follows a tight pattern. Pin the service. Pin the location. Show local credibility. Use extensions to make the ad bigger and more functional.

Ad Copy Best Practices

  • Highlight unique selling points and competitive advantages
  • Headline 1: Service or product focus, pinned to position 1. For single keyword ad groups, this should be the ad group name.
  • Headline 2: Location-based messaging (e.g., "Proudly Serving Houston"), pinned to position 2
  • Use Action-Oriented CTAs that encourage direct action (e.g., "Call Now")
  • Avoid excessive use of CTAs to avoid repetition in ad text. Recommend use in 1 headline and 1 description max.
⚠️ For Near Me Ads

Remove "Near Me" from the ad text and utilize target market location client services. The keyword is "near me" but the ad should still display the actual city or service area. "Near Me" in the headline reads awkwardly to a user — they want to see the real location of the business.

Ad Extensions to Use

Display address for store (Business Profile) — pulls from the linked GBP.

📞

Call Extensions — utilize CallRail Tracking number so calls are attributed to paid.

🔗

Section 6

Location Targeting & Audience Segmentation

Location targeting is where the campaign structure meets the real world. Get this wrong and the entire program suffers — get it right and the rest is fine-tuning.

CampaignLocation Targeting
Local Geo ModifiedSpecific cities, towns, or regions
National Geo ModifiedUSA & exclude specific cities, towns or regions
Near MeSpecific cities, towns, or regions

Audience Segmentation

  • Use Google Ads Audience Insights for demographic targeting
  • Leverage in-market audiences and customer match for tailored ads
⚠️ Audience Layering Warning

Consider how targeted this will make our ads as this could result in Low Search Volume if these are layered on top of the Local Location Targeting Structure. On small local budgets, layering audience segments on top of already-narrow geo targeting frequently produces "low search volume" alerts and starves the campaign. Use audience segmentation thoughtfully — usually as a bid modifier or for retargeting, not as a hard layer on top of geo.

Section 7

Mobile, Bidding & Call Tracking

Three operational areas that are easy to overlook but consistently move local performance.

📱

Mobile Optimization

  • Ensure mobile-responsive landing pages for fast load times and seamless user experience
  • Use click-to-call buttons to facilitate conversions directly from ads
  • Ensure use of correct PPC CallRail tracking numbers
💵

Bidding Strategy

  • Manual CPC Bidding provides the most control over bids and is a great starting point for a new program until data has time to collect
  • Use Maximize Conversions or Target ROAS to automate bids efficiently — once you have conversion data
📞

Call Tracking Integration

  • If client has CallRail, utilize appropriately assigned CallRail tracking numbers (i.e. Google PPC and Microsoft/Bing PPC numbers)
  • If client does not have CallRail, utilize their business phone number
Cross-Channel Synergy

Integrate with social media platforms like Facebook Ads to reach local audiences. Leverage Yelp, Local Service Ads, and Google Business Profiles to enhance visibility across platforms. Local Services Ads (LSAs) in particular are worth evaluating for service-area trades — they show above standard Search results and operate on a pay-per-lead model rather than pay-per-click.

Section 8

Testing & Continuous Optimization

Local paid is never "set it and forget it." But it's also not the place for huge experimental swings on tight budgets. Test small, measure, and move.

🎯 Differentiation

Emphasize local presence and unique service offerings.

📝 Ad Copy Testing

Experiment with local messaging and CTAs to improve engagement.

📄 Landing Page Testing

Optimize pages for local relevance and mobile performance.

📊 Continuous Optimization

Monitor performance routinely and adjust bids, keywords, or targeting based on results.

Section 9

Common Mistakes to Avoid

The specific paid-side errors that show up most often in new-teammate accounts.

The MistakeWhat to Do Instead
Running a single national campaign with city names in keywordsUse the three-campaign structure. Local Geo for in-area, National Geo for out-of-area intent for in-area locations, Near Me for device-driven local.
Skipping campaign-level negative keyword work at launchBuild a complete state/region negative list before the campaign goes live. Use Google or ChatGPT to generate the full state list with abbreviations.
Targeting ZIP codes for granular controlStick with cities, towns, and regions. ZIP-level targeting suppresses volume to the point of starving the campaign.
Using "Near Me" in actual ad copyThe keyword can be "near me" but the ad copy should show the real service location. Pin the city name in Headline 2.
Layering tight audience segments on top of tight geo targetingOn small budgets, audience layering causes "low search volume" issues. Use audiences as bid modifiers, not hard filters.
Forgetting to swap to the correct CallRail tracking number per channelGoogle PPC and Microsoft/Bing PPC should each have their own CallRail number for clean attribution.
Going straight to Smart Bidding on a brand-new account with no dataStart with Manual CPC until you have meaningful conversion data, then transition to Maximize Conversions or Target ROAS.
